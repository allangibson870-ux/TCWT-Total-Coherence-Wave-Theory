# TCWT Stress Test: Cosmological Expansion

## The Hypothesis
As the universe expands, the **Background Hum** density decreases. **TCWT** predicts that the "Universal Stiffness" ($\kappa$) is not a static constant across eons, but evolves with the scale of the universe.

## The Result
This simulation reveals a **"Coherence Expansion"**:
- **The Past**: In the early universe, $\kappa$ was higher, meaning the **Heisenberg Cut** happened much sooner. Atoms were "stiffer" but harder to keep in superposition.
- **The Future**: As $\kappa$ softens, the maximum volume for quantum coherence *increases*. The universe becomes "easier" to inhabit for quantum waves as it thins out.

## The "Dark Energy" Connection
In TCWT, **Dark Energy** is the pressure exerted by the "un-computed" portions of the wave function as they drift apart. It is the force of the Hum trying to maintain its /r^4$ equilibrium against expansion.
