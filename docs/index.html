Total Coherence Wave Theory: A Unified Phase-Field Framework for Matter, Gravity, and Cosmic Structure

Author: Allan Gibson

Year: 2026

Abstract

The Total Coherence Wave Theory (TCWT) proposes that the universe is permeated by a global oscillatory background—the Hum—whose phase structure underlies all physical phenomena. Matter appears as localized, solitonic distortions of this field, while gravity emerges from nonlinear phase-gradient bleed rather than curvature of spacetime or the presence of invisible mass. A single phase-field Lagrangian produces Newtonian gravity, MOND-like deep-space scaling, finite matter knots, gravitational lensing, Bullet Cluster separation, and strong-field orbital precession. Dark matter arises as an emergent effect of nonlinear gradient amplification, and dark energy appears as a small ghost-leakage pressure in the Hum vacuum. This paper presents the conceptual foundations, mathematical structure, and observational consequences of TCWT, offering a unified and non-singular alternative to both General Relativity and ΛCDM.
