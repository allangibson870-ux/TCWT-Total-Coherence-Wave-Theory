## 1. What the SCE Actually Is
The SCE upgrades the theory from a static checker to a **dynamical system**. Inconsistencies are treated as fields that propagate, renormalize, or vanish under scale flow. Instead of asking if reductions match, we ask: *"How does the local mismatch evolve?"*

## 2. Core Object: The Consistency Field
Define the **Consistency Field** $\Xi_{ij}(x,k)$:
*   $i,j$: Physics regimes (GR, QM, MOND, Cosmology, Fermions)
*   $x$: Spacetime coordinates
*   $k$: Coarse-graining scale

$\Xi_{ij}$ measures the local disagreement between any two physics reductions.

## 3. Definition of Mismatch Generation
For two reductions $S_i = R_i(S)$ and $S_j = R_j(S)$, the mismatch field is defined as:
$$\Xi_{ij} = R_i(S) - R_j(S)$$

## 4. SCE Evolution Equation (Core Result)
**Master Equation:**
$$\frac{\partial \Xi_{ij}}{\partial \ln k} = -\Gamma_{ij} \Xi_{ij} + S_{ij}[S] + N_{ij}(\Xi)$$

**Term Meanings:**
1.  **Decay Term ($-\Gamma_{ij} \Xi_{ij}$):** Drives RG stability; ensures IR agreement in well-behaved regimes.
2.  **Source Term ($S_{ij}[S]$):** Fundamental mismatch injection generated by MOND nonlinearities, ghost coupling, and Hopfion topology.
3.  **Nonlinear Backreaction ($N_{ij}(\Xi)$):** Represents mismatch feedback, typically modeled as $\lambda \Xi^2 - \eta \nabla^2 \Xi$.

## 5. Stability Condition
A TCWT pair of regimes is consistent **iff**:
$$\lim_{k \to 0} \Xi_{ij}(x,k) = 0 \quad \text{and} \quad \Xi_{ij} \text{ remains bounded for all } k$$

## 6. Fixed Points of Consistency Flow
*   **(A) Stable Fixed Point (Unified Theory):** $\frac{\partial \Xi}{\partial \ln k} = 0, \Xi = 0$. GR, QM, and MOND are perfectly consistent.
*   **(B) Marginal Fixed Point:** $\Xi \rightarrow \text{constant}$. Theory works but requires specific parameter tuning.
*   **(C) Runaway Phase:** $\Xi \rightarrow \infty$. Theory breaks down; missing counterterms or hidden degrees of freedom.

## 7. Upgraded Closure Condition
Instead of static matching $[R_i, R_j]S = 0$, we require:
$$\Xi_{ij}(x,k) \xrightarrow{k \to 0} 0 \quad \text{and remains bounded for all } k.$$
